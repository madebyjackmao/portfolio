About This Project

Every spring, Toronto's High Park transforms for a brief, fleeting window — cherry blossoms that last no more than two weeks before the petals fall. This project is an attempt to hold that moment still; a short video piece documenting the 2026 bloom season in the city I call home.

The work will combine slow cinematography, ambient sound, and a quiet editorial sensibility — more meditation than documentary. It is, in part, a response to the impermanence at the heart of all the other work in this portfolio: illustration fades, photographs age, games get patched. But a blossom is honest about how short it lasts.
