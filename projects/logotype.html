GLYPH

A collection of logotypes designed across 2025 — each one a small puzzle. How much can a single mark carry?

Overview

The Logotype Collection gathers a year's worth of identity work — personal projects, briefs, and experiments in brand mark design. Each logotype was approached as its own contained challenge: a single mark that needs to carry personality, scalability, and memorability all at once.

The range spans wordmarks, lettermarks, and abstract symbols — from restrained geometric forms to expressive hand-influenced designs.

Process

Every mark starts with the same question: what is the one thing this brand needs to feel? Trustworthy, playful, authoritative, warm — before a single line is drawn, that feeling has to be clear.

From there, the process is iterative and often destructive — drawing dozens of directions, discarding most, and refining the few that survive contact with critique. The hardest part is always knowing when to stop.

Reflection

Logo design is the most ruthlessly edited form of visual communication. Everything non-essential gets stripped away until only the core remains. It's taught me more about reduction and clarity than any other discipline I've worked in.
