A mark is never just a mark. It carries the weight of everything the designer believes — about clarity, about restraint, about what it means to make something that lasts.

About This Project

I've always been drawn to the edges of things — the point where a shape stops, the moment a letterform commits to its direction. This collection is a reflection of that instinct. Each mark was built from a single question: what is the most honest version of this idea?

The work spans wordmarks, lettermarks, and a custom typeface — all united by a belief that good design doesn't announce itself. It simply feels right, immediately and permanently. Toronto-made, globally minded, and always in pursuit of the detail that most people would never notice but everyone would feel.

JM Serif — original display typeface · 2026

The Typeface

Every collection needs a voice. For this one, I built one from scratch. JM Serif is a custom display typeface designed in collaboration with Claude AI — built on the Didot/Bodoni axis, the same historical foundation that informs the most iconic fashion mastheads in the world.

The brief I gave myself was simple: elegant, wide, and unforgettable. The result is a face with extreme thick-thin contrast, sharp unbracketed serifs, wide tracking, and an italic cut that flows with quiet confidence. It was designed to live in headlines and wordmarks — never apologetic, always composed.

Process

The typeface began as a conversation — about what I find beautiful, what I find lasting, and what I wanted my name to look like on a page. The design process was iterative and honest: I gave direction, pushed back on what felt generic, and kept refining until the letters felt like mine.

Working with AI as a design collaborator is still relatively new territory. What I found is that the creative instinct still has to come from you — the tool only goes where you point it. Every decision about weight, spacing, and terminal treatment was a deliberate choice rooted in my own aesthetic sensibility.

Reflection

This project sits at the intersection of everything I care about as a designer — the precision of letterform, the patience of brand thinking, and the honesty of knowing when to stop. Building a typeface, even a simple one, forces you to confront every assumption you hold about what makes a letter feel right.

Working with AI as a collaborator on something this personal was an interesting experience. It didn't give me the typeface — it helped me find it. The ideas, the taste, and the instinct all had to come from somewhere real. That somewhere is me.
