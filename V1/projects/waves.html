A study in visual tension — where rigid symmetry meets deliberate disruption. This project explores what happens when order is pushed just far enough to break.

Overview

Symmetrically Shorebound began as an experiment in formal constraint. The premise was simple: start with perfect bilateral symmetry, then introduce controlled chaos — each decision made with intention, never at random.

The result is a series of illustrations that feel simultaneously balanced and unsettled. Viewers often report a sense of recognition that slips just out of reach — familiar forms made strange by subtle deviations.

Process

The work started in pencil — loose gestural forms that were then refined digitally in Figma and Procreate. Each composition went through at least three symmetry passes before the deliberate breaks were introduced.

Color was the last decision. The palette draws from deep navy and violet, punctuated by the occasional warm accent — a coral or amber note that interrupts the cool equilibrium.

Final composition
Reflection

This project reinforced something I keep returning to: constraints are generative. The symmetry rule didn't limit the work — it gave it structure to push against. The most interesting moments happened right at the edge of the rule breaking.
